About SEMLAB

The Surface Electromagnetics Lab (SEMLAB) is a pioneering research facility dedicated to advancing the frontiers of electromagnetics through cutting-edge innovation and multidisciplinary collaboration. Located at the University of Surrey, SEMLAB focuses on designing and developing advanced technologies that shape the future of wireless communication, sensing, and beyond.

Our Primary Research Areas

  • Metamaterials and Metasurface Engineering: Pioneering new dimensions of engineered surfaces, SEMLAB was the first in the UK to demonstrate reconfigurable reflecting surfaces for 6G networks, providing groundbreaking solutions to coverage challenges.
  • Antennas and Arrays: Driving innovation in antenna design, we develop compact, efficient, and high-performance antennas for a wide range of applications, including mobile communications and space exploration.
  • Terahertz (THz) Technology: Bridging the spectral gap, our work in THz technology addresses critical challenges and explores applications in biomedicine, security, and next-generation wireless communications.
  • Wave Propagation: Enhancing the understanding of channel characteristics from sub-6 GHz to THz frequencies, we aim to optimize future 6G network performance through state-of-the-art propagation modeling.

Equipped with advanced facilities like an anechoic chamber, a hybrid beamformer testbed, and a terahertz spectroscopy system, SEMLAB supports groundbreaking experiments and applications. Our collaborative efforts with academic, industrial, and governmental entities ensure impactful contributions to the wireless communication and electromagnetics landscape.

At SEMLAB, we are committed to excellence in research, fostering innovation to address some of the most pressing technological challenges of our time.
